Masala Library

Masala Library is a huge collection of finesse given with how the restaurant has stood the testing waters of this industry and carved a founding base of modern craft and food.
The ambience I felt is soothing and oozes a charm yet I felt they could have done a better with the interiors. Nevertheless, its still one of the good ones out there allowing you to hug its comfort and never let it go.
We were served a 8 course meal, each and the kitchen duty for the night was being handled by Chef Anand. This meal also called as Chef's tasting menu priced at Rs. 2500+ GST for vegetarian.
The course started with Amouse Bouche which comprised of a mango jelly and coconut reduction being showcased as if one is eating eggs. Vegetarian eggs I would say.
Moving on to snacks or titbits, the best was Pumpkin Seeds which are served with a tiny pot of kokum fizz. The pumpkin seeds were lightly fried and tossed in a Tangy Masala and the Kokum Fizz was light, a bit sour and didn't hurt my vocals at all.
Tomato soup
was served alas a lavaash/bread but the soup was thick and so refreshing with the tanginess of tomatoes blending perfectly with low fat cream.
They made mushrooms so iconic and pairing it just rightly with assam tea, even actors stop by just to have this soup or chai, should I say. Button mushrooms are dehyrated and then poured with hot broth of assam chai and truffle oil powder.
Appetizers started with a Chevre Kebab. Softer than your skin cottage cheese, crumb fried with a touch of honey mixed alongside. The kebab was not upto the mark, with complete blandness cascading the required taste. Special mention though for the texture and the crumb, its difficult to fry paneer so well and crisp.
Pulled Jackfruit was tossed with spices, grounded masalad along with cashews~ it was good but not appealing to masses. Corn khees popper also was a failure or I would say, my taste bud was more deviated towards simple corn which is fried to a very crispy crunch.
Yam Kebab was the star dish. Given a smooth texture on the lines of galauti kebab, it had all my eyes, hands, ears and all body parts. Delicate yet the saunth chutney takes it a notch high.
Mains started with Dal Dhokla~ a very miserable attempt to merge gujarati food. Highly sweet and accentuated with complete lack of spices, I did raise my concern with the chef. Paneer Malwani Curry was the star pick~ I was mesmerized by how the chef balanced a spicy coastal curry to suite taste buds of foreign crowd. Red chillies are minimal but what turns around the dish is ample usage of finely powdered black pepper. Yes, its a dish worth all the price and accolades. Papad Ki Sabzi was another fantastic preparation and the sweet churma was quite an addiction.
Desserts steal all the limelight. Jalebi Cavair is the popular dessert. I dived for the rabdi and yes, it had me completely in awe for the chef. Bibinca is an Goan delicacy which served with a jamun mousse and paprika dust. Jamun Mousse was literally air, so light and paired so well with Bibinca!
The drinks, however were a complete let down. I think the bar tender seriously needs a lesson or two.
In all, Masala Library does stand tall still and definitely the chic place where Indian food finally gets its much needed recognition and accolades!

Masala Library is a vast collection of finesse. The interiors are so warm that you would want to hug in its entirety and yet, never let it go! . .
