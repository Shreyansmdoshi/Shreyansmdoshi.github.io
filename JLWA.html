THE MUNCHY BRISK

JLWA

From the stables of the same company as the one behind LOTD, Tamasha etc comes another big budget surprise. Opulence and grandeur rightly defined and maintained.
It's luxury at its best. Sharp detailing right from tables to its lighting. Aptly spaced out and after 11p, some of the tables make way for dance floor.
Chefs here; take a bow. Every dish you send across is just neatly detailed. The plates are beautiful and for a moment it feels like being part of a caricature which engulfs comics, papers and the whole city!
Drinks:
1. Talli Papai:
Fresh papaya chunks, papaya juice, tequila and thyme. For summers this drink is just the need of hour~ papaya keeps you hydrated, tequila gives you the kick and overall you will feel good because its starkingly different. No extra syrup, no extra sugar. Also, its magic!
2. Maple Whisky:
Whisky, kahlua, apple juice, maple syrup, tea, bitters and some drama smoke. It leans towards sweeter side but yes, a good juice for vegetarians. May I say, it exemplfies your class while on a date.
3. Bellini:
A classic cocktail which was just made right. Sets your mood in such way that your feet will hit the dance floor and mood catch the right notes.
4. Hello Darling:
Another drink which is beyond your usual cocktails. The pick here is fresh grapes, white rum, elderflower and a splah of champagne. Must have. The mouthfeel is fresh and because of such ingredients, perfect to cool down your day in summer.
Food:
1. Batata Bravas:
Baby potatoes batter fried served on a bed of tomato concasse and topped with peri peri cheese sauce. Its your tapas and quantity suffices 3-4 people. Neatly presented. If your drink is sweet, this is the right dish to order.
2. Tandoori Soya Chaap:
Moverover your regular paneer and indulge your glutton in the most sinful and fantastic tandoori dish. The soya chaaps are handfully crafted to such a great extent that even after 15 minutes of photographying it, chaaps were intact and didn't turn into its hard, rubber texture. The marination was spicy and tangy, flavourful at its core and when paired with mint chutney, you feel a moment of pride for ordering this dish. Served with baby garlic naans.
3. Mushroom Galouti Vol au Vent:
Miniscule galauti made from handpicked morels, spiced with cinnamon/clove/star anise and stuffed in baked paratha disc. I do not have a penchant for mushrooms yet I found the galouti to be soft and no need of chewing. Slightly bitter but the baked discs made it up. also, the papaya salad served on side is a treat in itself.
4. Dal Makhni:
Not heavily creamed but yes aple whole spices used and smoked. It is soothing and satiating. Served with pudina laccha paratha.
5. Classic Brownie:
Slightly dull (on the taste meter) and dry. Presentation personofies the opulence of restaurant though.
6. Lemon French Toast:
I would leave it upto you to order it rightaway.
Special mentions: Our server Aakash, is the coolest server we have come across yet.
P.S: We were invited for a tasting and the review has not been influenced by anyone.
