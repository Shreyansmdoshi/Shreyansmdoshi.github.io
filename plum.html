Cafe Plum

Restaurants are undergoing a drastic change and how! Chic, urbane and a more fashionable approach is in trend now. Plum by Bent Chair takes it a notch higher and presents itself as India's first retail restaurant.
Love the chairs, fork, spoon, plates, table, light so much so that you wish it to buy for your home? Well, Plum by Bent Chair makes it all possible! Serving a delectable Pan Asian food with exotic mushrooms given a huge preference in vegetarian menu, Plum is a certain visit!
Food:
1. Sesame Potato Toast:
Deep fried roundles of bread which is stuffed with a mash of potato and sprinkled with white and black sesame. Crispy and pairs excellently with their in-house made schezuan sauce.
2. Mushroom Broth:
Broth is a soup consisting of veggies or meat cooked in stock along with spices/herbs. A plethora of mushrooms replaces the veggies and the broth was fantastic with the scent of truffle oil viable at every sip.
3. Edamame and Truffle Wontons:
A pate or typically a mash of edamame mixed with cream cheese is stuffed in wonton sheets and then deep fried. I completely missed the aroma of truffle yet, the wontons were satisfying for a start. Served with super thin deep fried flat noodles.
4. Spring Rolls:
Button mushrooms along with Shimizu, crispy scallions are stuffed inside a phyllo sheet and then fried. The consistent layer and texture throughout and combined with the earthy prowess of mushrooms made it one of the exciting dishes of the night.
5. Cucumber, Quino and Avocado Salad:
It was highly refreshing. Crunchy and the lemon soy vinaigrette makes it a flavoursome dish. Order this one to balance the other dishes.
6. Sweet Potato Dimsums:
The natural sweetness of sweet potato was absent, therefore undermining the entire dish.
7. Edamame and Truffle Dimsums:
The same mixture of wontons is stuffed inside a layered starch and steamed.
8. Crispy Waterchestnuts:
Fried to a very good crunch, the spicy sauce in which they were tossed in lets the waterchestnuts soak in the sauce in its entirety and you will be pestered to order more and more.
9. Fried Soft Tofu:
The name itself is an antithesis of each two words. Tofu, handrolled and then fried which is dunked in a soya served with chopped bird eye chilli and brocolli. This dish is perfect for those who are not looking for any extra calories.
10. East Asian Curry:
The least enjoyed dish on the table. Sticky rice was not sticky at all. Curry was a mixed bag- couldn't identify any particular ingredient which would make the curry shine in terms of taste and aroma.
11. Avocado Cheesecake:
Served with chinese fruits, the cheesecake made the night come to a memorable end. It's surely a must have. Avocado puree blends in so well with cheesecake. A slight vanilla essence. Soft and moist and goey. Hits the right tune of tastebuds.
Plum by Bent Chair has carved a niche and the theme reflects highly in its interiors. For vegetarians, they have focussed on a good range of food with emphasis given on exotic fungii. A certain visit for Pan Asian food!
