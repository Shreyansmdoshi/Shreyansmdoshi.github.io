Cafe Coffee Time

Salute was one of the earliest, family fine dine which served liquor in this area. A part of Salute has been converted to a more renowned format: that of a cafe bar. Does it stand apart? Not exactly, but considering there aren't many pubs or cafes around here, it does pull the crowd.

Drinks:
Red fruit glass
Pink Senorita #recommended
Kitkat and Mocha Chip Shake #recommended
Food:
1. Crispy Lotus Stem:
Lotus stem fried to a crunch, tossed in medium spicy sauce. This is what any normal recipe related to the name of this dish. The chef goes beyond and places each lotus stem on a white bread! Yes! Lotus stem on a bread! Sounds disastrous and to an extent it was. Lotus stem was crisp but the bread was dry and I still cannot figure out why. You cannot everytime 'chef's recipe'.
2. Bruschetta:
Sliced baguette loaf and loaded with spicy beans and cheese. Except that the bread couldn't hold the weight of overloaded stuffing, the taste was very good and rightly enjoyable.
3. Penne in Pink Sauce:
Penne were overdone. The sauce was tangy and at perfect level of sweetness but since the pasta were overboiled, it led to a dismisal affair. Penne couldn't hold the sauce. Chef take a note!
Remember our good ol' bars which served peanuts and chakna along with beer and we as kids used to gobble it up? They still serve this.
For the pricing part and quantity, CCT is a good option in this area. Shakes are good and variety is quite big. The food; I would say needs bit more improvement.
