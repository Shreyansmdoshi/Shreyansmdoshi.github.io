Tanatan

Tanatan can be described the sound of bell in a holistic place frequented by religious sects, worshippers and followers.

Tanatan in Juhu is the synonym for the epitome that the food is~ its a place to be frequented by worshipper and followers of food.
The interiors are plush and the ambience is a sophistication wrapped around various artefacts and a stage for live band.

Food:
1. Sarson Brocolli:
Straight to the point, brocolli deserved more attention to marination and the dryness that followed. There was a complete apathy towards the sharp taste of kasundi. Presentation is spot on, but nothing fruitful comes out of it.
2. Paneer Tikka:
One appetizer which i was immensely not looking forward to be served. The marination was rich and overall, it was good with the paneer melting in mouth.
3. Khow Suey Samosa:
Phyllo sheets stuffed with noodles, veggies and galangal/lemongrass/onions/peppers. The taste was completely biased towards Thai cuisine, couldn't feel the Burmese Khow Suey feeling at all. Crispy and served with honey chilli sauce.
4. Dal Vadai Chattar Pattar:
The only saviour in the small bite section. 4 bite sized dal vada which are served with hummus. Hummus was fairly decent and complimented the dal vadai perfectly.
5. Lassoni Palak Paneer:
Served with laccha paratha, the quantity suffices two and was the best mains of the night. Thick and creamy gravy and the burnt garlic taste takes it a notch higher.
6. Mangal Pandey ki Pasand:
Chole which were given the authenticity taking back to you to Amritsar, loved the texture and the fact that it was light, spiced rightly and balanced adequately with ghee. Stuffed kulcha was good too and I couldn't stop myself from having it
7. Dal Khichdi with Schezwan:
A regular helping of dal khichdi. The twist here is instead of ghee, they did top it with schezuan chutney. Gave it a nice kick and the dish you will feel comfort at. A bit of sweetness was tasted.
8. Paneer Makhni, Dal Makhni:
Both were ferociously sweet. One bite and I left.
9.Subz Biryani:
The aroma engulfed the table. The taste left us unimpressed. Again feriously sweet. Chef take a note.
10. Desserts were completely good and made smile a bit. Balanced just fine in terms of sugar, my favourite was the Rabdi, Mango Sorbet with Brownie Crumbles, Chocolate Fondant and mango custard cake.
Drinks:
Cocktails were highly diluted, while the mockatils were fresh and we felt that they could have bought some goood inovations and increase Value For Money quotient. The only favourite on the table was the Paan ki Shaan, a drink made of vodka, paan and spices. Refreshing for summer and a feel good mouthfeel.
Overall, Tanatan makes more noise than it should. The chef needs to improve the balance of flavours and adjust the sweetness and redo the dryness of appetizers.

One of the best Paan based drink ever had. Strong and super refreshing.
