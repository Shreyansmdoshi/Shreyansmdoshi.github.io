Banana Leaf

Soothing calmness. Ritualistic vigour. Bouts of greenery along the highways and the humidity in the weather~ south India does define a holistic approach to what a real vacation is.
And when you get a part of it in our own megacity of Mumbai, do you really want to miss it? Certainly not. I was also a bit sceptical since my parents had earlier visited their Borivali branch and their experience didn't turn out to be as pleasant as mine. It so seems they now need to focus on standardization across their branches.
Food:
1. Coorgi Idli:
Quantity just enough for two. Infact if one person is way too hungry, quantity will just suffice him/her. Idlis cut into pieces and are then tossed in a spicy mix of tomatoes/onions and garlic. Not so spicy as it looks yet definitely tasty and hot.
2. Mulgai Idli:
Chopped idlis tossed in a special grinded spice mix of mint/coriander/green chillies. The spice mix was smooth and had ample natural spice from green chillies yet I found it Coorgi Idli more enjoyable.
3. Nuppu Bonda:
I can term this as south indian arancini. Balls made of rice, chillies, coriander which is stuffed with processed cheese. A dish so simple yet made with equal love. Its lipsmacking and you will be left to order more.

4. Podi Uttampam:
The centre was fluffy and soft. The edges perfectly crisp. Ghee laden and podi masala giving it a perfect taste (course and spicy). I couldn't take my hands of this dish.
5. Madhur Vadai:
Commonly known as dal vada. Its subtle and light. Not heavily fried. Crunchy throughout and onions balancing out the rawness from dal.

6. Paneer Chettinad & Appam:
Paneer cubes tossed in a rich chettinad gravy (they use coconut milk to enhance the rich texture and not cream). Nicely balanced out with spicy/sweet taste consistent throughout. Appams were soft and fluffy.
7. Bissi Bele Rice:
From the state of karnataka, its a typical khichdi with some veggies, tadka of ghee and curry leaves. What sets bissi bele rice is the consistency; you need not chew it. There's tange flavour to it equally balanced by earthy flavours of dal (chana nd urad). The restaurant had put good use of ghee in this. Often served with a crispy element, we had a popadum to eat along.
8. Payassam:
How about me not describing it and you just ordering this? Its super good with the acidity of milk rightly cutting across the sweetness and almonds giving you a nutty flavour.
Overall, though my experience was far better, considering my family's personal experience too, I would go rating it 4/5. The quantity except for the rice plate, is manageable by only two people. The food and service however is detailed and fresh.
