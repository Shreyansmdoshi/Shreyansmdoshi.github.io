Sante Spa Cuisine

I had the healthiest (and tastiest) meal of my lifetime here few days back. Yes, healthy and tasty do not go hand in hand right? Trust Santé Spa Cuisine to do the honours and calm you in the soothing and laidback atmosphere of the restaurant.
For all the bonkers and noises, this place isn't for you. The music here is calming and evokes the deepest senses of your soul. The food here is all natural with bare minimums being freshly ordered from sellers around here. The owner has a farm, wherein she produces the raw materials. Such is the love around here, that the cutlery has been handcrafted from neem wood, the purple rice is imported from a North Eastern State, the spices from Kerala etc. The list is endless like their menu; which tosses your head in a storm spin until the server is ready to give you a hand and grab you out. Yes, the service is top notch.
We started our #honestlyhealthy meal with shots of Activated Charcoal lemonade and Vegan Turmeric Coco latte. The lemonade had hints of pink salt and was refreshing. Latte was my favourite; Subtle spice of turmeric blending with natural sweetness of coconut. A mouth-feel which is cooler than menthol.
Appetizers:b>
Southern Spiced cottage cheese: Cottage cheese marinated in fresh grounded spices, grilled along with curry leaves and served with watermelon chutney. It’s spicy and the chutney balances the dish perfectly. A simple dish made extravagant by balancing it perfectly.
Hummus and Khakhra:
Moving over pita, we have spinach and beetroot hummus which are served with khakhra. The hummus was very good with very strong taste of garlic. Ragi khakhras turn out to be the best compliment to chunky hummus.
Bread basket:
Consisting of oat rolls, multigrain mini ciabatta, wheat bran baps, and lavash along with flavoured butters. My pick was the garlic butter. All butters are made in-house with no preservatives.
Chlorophyll Dumplings:
Broccoli, pokchoy, baby spinach encased in a tapioca sheet and then steamed. It’s so soft and seemingly hot. Served with a ginger scallion sauce, I was in awe as to how all the greens systematically flavour each other and makes the dumplings stand out. Special mention for a slight drop of chlorophyll used.
Soups:
Watermelon Gazpacho:
I love gazpacho simply because it’s a cold broth. I have had pumpkin gazpacho at another outlet and it was outstanding. Here, the gazpacho uses watermelon along with some mint drops. Consistency was good but the over usage of black pepper marred the watermelon down.
Thukpa:
Soba noodles and Asian greens in a spicy, soya broth. A spoon of this is a must in this weather. It’s relaxing and the Chinese five spice gives your soul the healing required.
Pizza:
Pizza Verde and Mix Veg pizza:
The pizza base is made of ragi and 100% whole wheat. Thin as a pin, it doesn't get soggy and the base is able to hold the weight of sauce, cheese and veggies. Pizza Verde has pesto as its base complimented by cashew cheese, cheddar and all greens. It might seem to be not so appetizing, but you ought to order this over the Mix veg pizza.
Mains:
Purple Risotto:
Purple rice imported from North East India served with morning glory and crunchy carrots. A bowl of green Thai curry is served to compliment the rice. I couldn’t fathom even a spoon of this dish. Since I am not averse to the taste of purple rice, it was not to my liking.
Spaghetti with Kale pesto:
Spaghetti is whole wheat and is tossed with buttered veggies served on a pesto made from kale, coconut milk. Its light and the kale pesto is a different universe from the traditional basil pesto. Do order this one.
Broccoli Steak:
A whole broccoli is oven roasted served on a bed of tomato concasse served with creamy sauce and potato foam. If the potato foam was the star element, broccoli was roasted without salt, meaning we were left lurching for that spicy tomato concasse and creamy sauce. Again, the creamy sauce is made from cashew milk and has white butter in it for consistency.
Desserts:
Almond Muesli Ice-cream:
Low fat milk and cream, almond, a touch of saffron and crunchy muesli combined to give you a luscious mouth feel and a never ending order spree.
Matcha Truffles:
These mini bombs satiate your hunger for all things sweet. The Matcha flavour is spot on delicately mixed with dark chocolate.
Salted Caramel Pastry:
Made with oatmeal and 100% wheat flour, this is was the star dish of the day. Soft and just melt in mouth. Salted caramel is not excess, yet the pink salt gives the brown sugar caramel a twist and a treat to your tongue.
Special Mentions:
The owner and her team for the efforts taken to make us feel a part of her journey, her thoughts regarding natural well -being, research about the best available source for raw produce etc. (Long list :p)
